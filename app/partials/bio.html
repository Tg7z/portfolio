More about me

I was born and raised in New Zealand but in late 2013 I decided it was finally time to fly the coop and discover what it's like on the other side of the world.

Having spent two years in employment at E-see International I had learnt a lot and my career direction was now pointing in a different to what it had when I started there.

Initially I joined e-see® with a design oriented education and a focus on brand. They were the perfect company for me to start my formal employment with as the core of their buisness was a Brand Asset Management app. As my role progressed I was spending more time working on web projects and developed my skills in web coding. Initially this was more advanced HTML and CSS but as time went by I upskilled into JavaScript.

I found myself enjoying the challenges of designing and coding for the web. There was also the darkside, however, I became much more aware of and frustrated by counter-intuitive user experiences. I hope to contribute to solutions that are not only beautiful but also intuitive for end users and easily maintainable for future developers.

I now have a keen interest in crafting intuitive and rich user experiences and am very intrigued by the potential of next generation client-side MV* frameworks such as AngularJS and Ember.

I am currently continuing to provide support to e-see® as an external contractor. I am also working on upskilling myself by improving my code knowledge and learning AngularJS.

Didacticode?

This blog's name Didacticode comes from the conjugation of the words didactic, which means "intended to teach" and code. This blogs intention is primarily to teach myself and others about web coding.

The blog was originally created due to the need for a portfolio. I thought to myself, what better way to present portfolio work and give prospective employers insight into what makes the developer inside me tick than a blog. I could put projects on a timeline for the portfolio and blog about changes in the industry and technologies I am adopting as a way to digest the information and show my professional development.
